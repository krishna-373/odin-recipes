Pasta Shapes



There are many different types of pasta, each with its own unique shape and texture. Here are some common pasta shapes:

  • Spaghetti
  • Fusilli
  • Penne
  • Farfalle
  • Macaroni
  • Tagliatelle
  • Linguine
  • Ravioli
  • Tortellini

Each pasta shape is suited for different types of sauces and dishes. For example, spaghetti is often paired with tomato sauce, while ravioli is typically served with a creamy sauce.

🍳 Instructions:
Boil the pasta:

In a large pot, boil water with 1 tsp salt.

Add pasta and cook as per packet instructions (usually 8–10 minutes).

Drain and set aside. Save 1/2 cup pasta water.

Prepare the sauce:

Heat olive oil in a pan.

Add chopped garlic and sauté till golden.

Add onion and sauté till translucent.

Add tomato puree and cook for 5–6 minutes until oil separates.

Add salt, pepper, chili flakes, and herbs.

Add cream (or milk) and mix well. Simmer for 2 minutes.

Mix pasta with sauce:

Add boiled pasta to the sauce.

Mix well and add a little reserved pasta water if it’s too thick.

Cook for 1–2 minutes until pasta is well coated.

Serve:

Top with grated cheese and fresh herbs.

Serve hot with garlic bread or a side salad.

Enjoy experimenting with different pasta shapes and sauces to create delicious meals!
